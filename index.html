About Me

I have been working in the area of management and prevention of childhood malnutrition, diarrheal diseases, and infectious illnesses for the last 14 years at icddr,b, Dhaka Medical College Hospital, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Medical University, and Dhaka Children's Hospital. I am part of the faculty of Public Health Nutrition at the BRAC James P. Grant School of Public Health (Bangladesh) and Uppsala University (Sweden). I am also involved in different training programs on nutrition and infectious diseases across the country. I am the principal investigator of the tebipenem-pivoxil clinical trial for shigellosis in children which is being conducted at icddr,b. I have a number of publications in peer-reviewed journals on infectious diseases and malnutrition.

Ongoing Projects

Tebipenem-pivoxil as an alternative to ceftriaxone for clinically non-responding children with shigellosis
Principal Investigator | 2021-2025
This project aims to determine whether tebipenem-pivoxil is non-inferior to the currently WHO-recommended second-line Shigella therapy (ceftriaxone). We have completed the pharmacokinetics trial and going to start the main trial soon.

Immune response in Shigella spp. Infected human colonic tissue using CyTOF and RNAseq
Co-investigator | 2019-2024
The aim of this study was to understand the host responses to S. flexneri infection in order to facilitate the development of potential host-directed therapeutics.

Completed Projects

Zinc sulfate acceptability study in children with acute diarrhea
Co-investigator | 2019-2020
The aim of the study was to evaluate the acceptability of the improvised zinc product in the management of childhood diarrhea. The findings showed that the modified Zinc formulation was well tolerated, and caregivers' willingness to use this formulation in the future supports its acceptability, adherence, and palatability.
account_balance ACME Laboratories LTD, Bangladesh
Pilot of a Prebiotic and Probiotic Trial in Young Infants with Severe Acute Malnutrition
Co-investigator | 2018-2020
The study aimed to evaluate the engraftment of Bifidobacterium infantis in the intestine of malnourished young infants with or without prebiotic supplements. This intervention increased fecal B. infantis abundance in infants with SAM, although levels were still 10- to 100-fold lower than in untreated healthy controls.
account_balance Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF)
Antibiotics for Children with Severe Diarrhoea (ABCD)
Co-investigator | 2017-2019
This was a randomized, placebo-controlled trial to determine if the addition of an antibiotic (azithromycin) to the standard management for acute non-bloody watery diarrhea in a subset of children 2-23 months of age who are dehydrated or undernourished could reduce the mortality and improve growth in settings where such deaths commonly occur.
account_balance Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF)
